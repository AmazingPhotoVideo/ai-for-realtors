# Home Valuation Funnel

A seller funnel that earns the lead by promising a human-reviewed value range, not a fake instant estimate.

## Funnel map

Ad/QR/email/post → valuation page → address + consent form → instant expectations message → CMA task → value email/video → appointment CTA → seller nurture.

## Offer positioning

Weak: “What is your home worth?”

Better: **“Get a realistic value range based on recent local sales, condition, and buyer demand — reviewed by a local agent.”**

## Landing page copy

Headline: **Find out what buyers would likely pay for your [AREA] home in today’s market**

Subhead: Automated estimates miss renovations, layout, lot, parking, views, building rules, and timing. Request a human-reviewed range using current comparable sales.

Bullets:
- Recent nearby sales, not national averages
- Adjustments for condition, layout, and upgrades
- Suggested pricing strategy if you plan to sell
- No obligation; useful even if you are 6–12 months out

Button: **Request my value range**

Form fields:
- Address
- Name
- Email
- Mobile
- Property type
- Updates/condition notes
- Timeline: now / 3–6 months / 6–12 months / researching
- Consent checkbox

## Confirmation copy

Thank-you page:

> Thanks — I’ll review your address against recent relevant sales. If anything is unclear, I may ask one or two questions so the range is not misleading.

SMS:

> Thanks for requesting a value range for [ADDRESS]. I’m reviewing local comps and will send the first pass shortly. Any major updates I should factor in? Reply STOP to opt out.

Email:

Subject: I received your value request for [ADDRESS]

Hi [FIRST],

I received your request. Automated estimates often miss the details that matter, so I’ll compare your home against recent relevant sales and note where condition, layout, parking, lot, or building factors may change the range.

If you have recent updates, reply with them and I’ll include them.

[AGENT]

## CRM fulfillment workflow

Tags: `home-valuation`, `seller-lead`, `area:[slug]`, `timeline:[bucket]`, `source:[utm]`.

1. Verify property details and ownership/solicitation rules.
2. Pull 3–6 comps: same building/street first, then broader area.
3. Note adjustments: size, beds/baths, condition, lot, parking, view, basement, fees.
4. Return a range with confidence level: high/medium/low.
5. Include next step: video walkthrough, prep checklist, net sheet, or pricing call.

## 14-day nurture

- Day 0: confirmation
- Day 1: send preliminary range or request missing info
- Day 3: “3 factors that could raise/lower your number”
- Day 7: seller prep checklist
- Day 14: invite to 15-minute pricing call
- Monthly: local market report

## Ads and retargeting

Creative angles:
- “Automated estimates miss renovations.”
- “See the sales buyers are comparing against your home.”
- “Planning a move in 6–12 months? Start with the number.”

Use housing-compliant targeting. Prefer first-party lists, broad/local geo where allowed, and contextual creative. Avoid demographic exclusion or language that targets protected classes.

## KPIs

- Landing conversion: 8–25% cold, 20–45% warm/sphere
- Contactability: 60%+ valid email/mobile
- CMA delivered within 24 hours
- Pricing-call booking: 5–15% of qualified requests
- Listing appointment: 2–8% depending on timeline

## Compliance and APV attribution

State that ranges are estimates, not appraisals. Do not guarantee sale price. Follow CASL/TCPA/PIPEDA and brokerage advertising policies.
