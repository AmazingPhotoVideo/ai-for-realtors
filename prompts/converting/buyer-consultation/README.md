# Buyer Consultation Script for the Post-NAR Settlement Environment

This is a modern buyer consultation script for agents who need to explain value, agency, compensation, search strategy, and next steps clearly before touring homes. It is written for the U.S. post-NAR-settlement environment and includes Canadian/global adaptation notes.

> This is training and marketing guidance, not legal advice. Use your brokerage forms, local association guidance, and counsel-approved language.

## Why this conversation changed

After the NAR settlement practice changes, many real estate professionals must have a written buyer agreement before in-person or live virtual home tours. The agreement should clearly describe services and compensation. Compensation is negotiable, and buyers should understand who pays, when it is paid, and what happens if seller-paid compensation or concessions are not available.

The consultation is no longer a casual pre-showing chat. It is where you earn trust before a buyer signs anything.

## Consultation goals

By the end of the meeting, the buyer should understand:

1. Their goals, timeline, buying power, and risk tolerance.
2. How agency/representation works in your jurisdiction.
3. What services you provide before, during, and after the purchase.
4. How compensation works, including negotiability and possible payment paths.
5. What written agreement is required before tours and what the key terms mean.
6. The exact next step: pre-approval, search setup, agreement, tour, offer prep, or nurture.

## Pre-call checklist

- Confirm whether the buyer is already represented.
- Verify consent for SMS/email reminders.
- Send the discovery form or use the discovery-bot skill.
- Pull sample listings in the target areas.
- Prepare lender intro options if appropriate.
- Have your brokerage buyer representation agreement and agency disclosure ready.
- Know local rules on showing agreements, touring, compensation language, rebates, and concessions.

## 45-minute agenda

| Minutes | Section | Outcome |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-5 | Rapport and purpose | Reduce pressure, set agenda |
| 5-15 | Goals and constraints | Understand motivation, timing, must-haves |
| 15-25 | Market and search strategy | Explain realities of inventory, pricing, competition |
| 25-35 | Representation and compensation | Explain services, agreement, negotiability |
| 35-42 | Buying plan | Financing, offer strategy, due diligence, timeline |
| 42-45 | Decision and next step | Agreement, lender intro, search, tour, follow-up |

## Script

### 1) Open with control and consent

"Thanks for taking the time. My goal today is not to pressure you into anything. It is to help you understand the buying process, what I do for you, how representation and compensation work, and whether it makes sense for us to move forward together.

Before we talk about specific homes, I want to ask about your goals and then I’ll explain the agreement required before tours so there are no surprises. Does that sound fair?"

### 2) Diagnose the buyer's real goal

Ask one at a time:

- "What made you start looking now?"
- "If this purchase goes well, what changes in your life?"
- "What would make you regret the purchase a year from now?"
- "Are you trying to buy by a date, by a budget, or only if the right property appears?"
- "What are the three non-negotiables?"
- "What are you flexible on if the perfect home does not exist?"
- "Are there any constraints I should know about: financing, commute, accessibility features, pets, selling a current home, lease timing, or family logistics?"

Fair-housing-safe redirect if needed:

"I can’t evaluate neighbourhoods based on who lives there or protected-class factors. What I can do is help you compare objective criteria like property type, commute, transit, amenities, budget, taxes/fees, and publicly available resources so you can make your own decision."

### 3) Confirm readiness

"Where are you with financing: fully pre-approved, pre-qualified, cash, need a lender introduction, or not started?"

If not ready:

"That is normal. Before we spend time touring, the best move is to understand buying power and monthly comfort. I can introduce you to a lender, or you can use your own. I do not want you falling in love with a home before the numbers are clear."

### 4) Explain your buyer service model

"Here is what I do for buyers beyond opening doors:

- Build the search around your goals, not just portal alerts.
- Verify listing status, property history, disclosures, taxes/fees, condo/HOA/strata details where available, and showing constraints.
- Help compare homes objectively so you do not overpay for the wrong trade-offs.
- Coordinate lenders, inspectors, lawyers/escrow/title, insurance, and deadlines.
- Prepare offer strategy: price, terms, contingencies/conditions, deposit/earnest money, closing date, inclusions, and negotiation approach.
- Protect your time and reduce avoidable risk from search through closing."

### 5) Post-NAR representation and compensation explanation

Use plain language and your brokerage-approved specifics:

"Before I can tour homes with you in many situations, we need a written buyer agreement. The point is transparency: what services I provide, how long the agreement lasts, what geographic/property scope it covers, and how compensation works.

Compensation is negotiable. Depending on the property and the offer strategy, it may be paid by the seller/listing side if offered, negotiated as a seller concession, paid directly by you, or handled through another brokerage-approved structure. We will review this before you sign so you know the maximum obligation and the scenarios that could happen.

What I will not do is tell you it is always free or hide the details until offer time. You deserve clarity before you rely on my advice."

### 6) Agreement walkthrough

Cover these terms in simple language:

- Parties: who represents whom.
- Scope: property type, geography, price range if applicable.
- Duration: start/end date and cancellation terms.
- Services: what the agent will do.
- Exclusivity/non-exclusivity if applicable.
- Compensation: amount/rate/method, whether negotiable, when due, how seller offers/concessions affect it.
- Buyer obligations: honesty, communication, working through the agent, timely documents.
- Agency disclosure, conflicts, dual/designated agency rules where applicable.

Questions to ask:

- "What part of this agreement feels unclear or uncomfortable?"
- "Would a shorter initial term help you evaluate the fit?"
- "Is your concern compensation, commitment, or trust?"

### 7) Market reality without fear tactics

"Here is what I’m seeing in {market} as of {date}: {verified_inventory_or_price_context}. That means our strategy should be {strategy}."

If no current data is available:

"I do not want to guess. I’ll pull current inventory, recent sales, and active competition before we choose specific homes."

### 8) Build the buying plan

Agree on:

- Target areas and acceptable substitutes.
- Search criteria and alert cadence.
- Financing/lender step.
- Agreement choice and tour readiness.
- Offer-prep threshold: "If we find the right home, what conditions must be true for you to write?"
- Communication channel and response expectations.

### 9) Close

If ready:

"Based on what you shared, I think the next step is {agreement/pre-approval/search/tour}. We can keep the initial scope focused on {scope} and review it as we go. If you are comfortable, let’s complete the paperwork, set up the search, and schedule the first tour."

If not ready:

"No problem. The useful next step is {lender intro/monthly search/market update/check-in date}. I’ll keep it practical and we can revisit representation before you start touring."

## Objection scripts

### "Why do I need to sign before seeing one house?"

"I understand why that feels like a lot for one showing. The rule is designed so buyers know who represents them and how compensation works before touring. We can keep the agreement narrow in scope or duration where allowed by brokerage policy, but we do need clarity before I provide representation or arrange tours."

### "Does this mean I have to pay your commission?"

"Not automatically. It means we agree in writing how compensation works and what the maximum obligation is. Depending on the property, compensation may be offered by the seller/listing side, negotiated in the offer, paid by you, or handled another approved way. We will discuss it property by property so there are no surprises."

### "Another agent said it costs me nothing."

"I would be careful with that wording. Sometimes buyers do not pay directly at closing because compensation is handled through the transaction, but it is still an economic term. I prefer to explain it clearly so you can compare options honestly."

### "I do not want to be locked in."

"That is reasonable. Let’s talk about scope, term, and cancellation. A good agreement should protect both of us without trapping you in a relationship that is not working."

## Canadian adaptation notes

For Canadian agents, replace NAR-specific language with your provincial real estate council/board requirements for agency disclosure, buyer representation agreements, FINTRAC where applicable, privacy/PIPEDA, CASL for electronic follow-up, and brokerage policy. Do not import U.S. settlement language into Canadian agreements unless your brokerage counsel approves it.

## Metrics to improve the consultation

- Consult-to-signed-agreement rate.
- Consult-to-pre-approval/lender-intro completion.
- Agreement objection rate by source.
- Tour show rate.
- Offer rate from represented buyers.
- Buyer satisfaction/review requests after closing.
- Deals lost to unclear compensation or delayed agreement conversation.
