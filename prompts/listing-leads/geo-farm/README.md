# Geo-Farm Content Calendar

Build a 90-day neighbourhood authority plan that earns seller conversations through consistency, local usefulness, and proof. A good farm does not feel like 90 days of “Call me for a free home evaluation.” It feels like the agent is documenting the market better than anyone else.

## Strategy principles

- **Narrow beats broad.** A 700-home farm you can touch every month is better than a 5,000-home farm you mention twice a year.
- **Local proof beats generic tips.** Use street-level sales, inventory, school catchments, condo stacks, lot types, renovations, and buyer objections.
- **Multiple channels compound.** Direct mail, email, social, search, YouTube, door knocks, open houses, and retargeting should all point to the same homeowner offers.
- **Seller value comes before seller ask.** Teach pricing, prep, timing, and net proceeds before asking for the appointment.
- **Measure by conversations, not likes.** The farm exists to produce valuation requests, listing appointments, referrals, and repeat recognition.

## Farm setup checklist

1. Define exact boundaries: streets, buildings, school zone, waterfront, subdivision, or price band.
2. Pull baseline numbers: homes, turnover rate, owner-occupancy estimate, average sale price, DOM, inventory, expireds, absentee owners if legally sourced.
3. Audit competitors: who already owns mindshare, where they advertise, what they say, how often they show up.
4. Pick three seller offers: valuation, prep checklist, and quarterly market report.
5. Build tracking: CRM tag, landing page, UTM, QR, call tracking, monthly dashboard.
6. Create repeatable content pillars: market data, homeowner education, local lifestyle, proof/case studies, listing leverage, community partnerships.
7. Set cadence: weekly social, monthly email, monthly/bi-monthly mail, quarterly report, daily/weekly prospecting block.

## Postcard concepts

1. **Quarterly snapshot:** “What changed in [FARM_NAME] this quarter?”
2. **Just sold lesson:** “What [ADDRESS/AREA] tells nearby owners.”
3. **Prep checklist:** “Before you spend $10,000 getting ready to sell…”
4. **Inventory alert:** “Buyers have [more/fewer] choices than last month.”
5. **Model match:** “Own a [MODEL/PROPERTY TYPE]? Here are the comps that matter.”
6. **Equity checkup:** “Your home value changed. Your plan should too.”

## Compliance cautions

- **US:** Follow Fair Housing, state advertising/license rules, brokerage approval, CAN-SPAM, TCPA, DNC, platform ad category rules, and current NAR settlement-era language around compensation. Avoid fixed commission claims and protected-class targeting.
- **Canada:** Follow CASL, PIPEDA/provincial privacy, National DNCL/CRTC rules, provincial regulator advertising requirements, brokerage approval, and FINTRAC obligations when applicable. Use REALTOR® marks only according to CREA rules.
- **Data:** Cite MLS/board data only as permitted. Some boards restrict public display of sold data, DOM, or aggregated stats. When unsure, use approved market summaries or anonymized examples.
