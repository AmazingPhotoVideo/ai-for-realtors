# $5/Day Buyer Lead Machine

A practical setup for generating buyer leads with a small daily budget, a useful lead magnet, fast follow-up, and simple retargeting. This is not a magic ad hack. It is a low-budget system for testing offers, collecting intent signals, and booking buyer consultations.

## What you will build

- One buyer lead magnet: first-time buyer roadmap, neighbourhood guide, investor calculator, or open-house property pack
- One mobile-first landing page
- One thank-you page with a consult CTA
- One Meta campaign at roughly $5/day
- One optional Google Search test if there is clear search demand
- One 7-email nurture
- One 5-message SMS follow-up
- One retargeting audience for site/video engagers where allowed
- One metrics dashboard

## Before you start

You need:

- A landing page tool or website page
- CRM or email platform
- SMS tool with compliant opt-in/opt-out support
- Meta Business account and pixel/dataset installed if available
- Google Analytics or another analytics tool
- Privacy policy linked from landing page
- Brokerage-approved ad disclaimers and license info
- A clear offer for one audience

## Step 1 — Choose the offer

Pick one. Do not run a generic “contact me to buy a home” campaign.

| Audience | Offer | Best CTA |
|---|---|---|
| First-time buyers | `[MARKET] First-Time Buyer Roadmap` | Download the roadmap |
| Neighbourhood researchers | `[NEIGHBOURHOOD] Buyer Guide` | Get the guide |
| Investors | Rental Deal Analyzer | Send me the calculator |
| Open-house visitors | Property Pack + Similar Listings | View property pack |
| Relocators | Moving to `[MARKET]` Guide | Get relocation guide |

The offer should be specific, local, and immediately useful. Hyperlocal guides, calculators, checklists, and short roadmaps tend to outperform broad ebooks because the lead knows exactly what they are getting.

## Step 2 — Build the landing page

Use this page structure:

```text
Hero headline: Get the [SPECIFIC OFFER] for [MARKET/AUDIENCE]
Subhead: One sentence explaining the concrete outcome.
Bullets: 4-6 practical things inside.
Visual: PDF mockup, checklist screenshot, neighbourhood map, calculator screenshot, or short agent video.
Form: first name, email, phone optional, timeline, buyer type.
Consent: email/SMS follow-up and STOP opt-out if texting.
CTA button: Send Me the [GUIDE/CHECKLIST/CALCULATOR]
Trust: agent name, brokerage, reviews/proof if approved.
FAQ: Who is this for? Is it free? Will you call me? What happens next?
Footer: privacy policy, brokerage/license, optional APV resource credit if appropriate.
```

### Landing page example

```text
Headline: Buy Your First Home in Toronto With a Clear 90-Day Plan
Subhead: Get the local roadmap for budget prep, closing costs, pre-approval, offer steps, and what to ask before touring.
Bullets:
- Estimate cash to close beyond the down payment
- Understand pre-approval and monthly payment ranges
- Learn what happens from offer to keys
- Ask better questions before your first showing
CTA: Send Me the Roadmap
```

## Step 3 — Create the thank-you page

Do not waste the thank-you page.

```text
Headline: Your guide is on the way.
Next step: Want a custom plan?
Body: If you share your budget, timeline, and preferred areas, [AGENT_NAME] can send a practical next-step list or shortlist.
Buttons:
- Book a 15-minute buyer consult
- Send me listings like this
- Ask a question by text
```

## Step 4 — Install tracking

Track at minimum:

- Landing page views
- Form submissions
- Thank-you page views
- Consult button clicks
- Phone/SMS clicks
- Email/SMS replies manually in CRM if needed

Use UTM parameters:

```text
utm_source=meta
utm_medium=paid_social
utm_campaign=buyer_[offer]_[market]
utm_content=[creative_angle]
```

## Step 5 — Build the follow-up

### Instant email

```text
Subject: Your [OFFER] is here
Hi [FIRST_NAME],

Here’s the [OFFER]: [LINK]

Start with [BEST_SECTION]. If you reply with your budget, timeline, and target areas, I’ll send a short next-step list.

— [AGENT_NAME]
[BROKERAGE]
```

### 7-email nurture

1. Instant: deliver the asset and ask one question.
2. Day 2: explain the biggest mistake for this audience.
3. Day 4: provide a checklist or calculator tip.
4. Day 7: show how to compare options.
5. Day 10: explain representation/offer readiness.
6. Day 15: invite them to send a listing or question.
7. Day 21: consultation CTA.

### 5-message SMS follow-up

```text
1. Hi [FIRST_NAME], it’s [AGENT_NAME]. I sent the [OFFER]. Are you researching or hoping to buy in the next 6 months?
2. Quick tip from the guide: [ONE USEFUL TIP]. Want me to send examples in [MARKET]?
3. If you send me a listing link, I’ll tell you what I’d verify before touring.
4. Are you comparing any specific neighbourhoods or property types?
5. Want a 15-minute buyer plan? Here’s my calendar: [CALENDAR_LINK]
```

Only send SMS where you have proper consent and opt-out handling.

## Step 6 — Launch the $5/day Meta test

### Campaign settings

- Objective: leads or sales/conversions depending on tracking maturity; traffic can work for early landing-page testing but optimize for leads as soon as possible.
- Category: select Housing/Special Ad Category where required.
- Budget: $5/day for 14 days minimum.
- Geography: use platform-compliant radius/region targeting.
- Audience: broad within permitted area; avoid restricted demographics and protected-class proxies.
- Placements: Advantage+ placements or feeds/reels/stories if creative is formatted correctly.
- Creative: 2-3 variants max at this budget.
- Destination: landing page or native lead form.

### Creative set

Run three angles:

1. **Problem:** “The down payment is not the only number.”
2. **Local:** “Buying in [NEIGHBOURHOOD]? Compare price, property type, commute, and monthly cost.”
3. **Tool:** “Run the numbers before you tour.”

### Example Meta ads

```text
Ad 1 — First-time buyer
Primary: Buying your first home in [MARKET]? Get the local roadmap for budget prep, closing costs, pre-approval, offers, and closing day.
Headline: First-Time Buyer Roadmap
CTA: Download

Ad 2 — Neighbourhood guide
Primary: Comparing homes in [NEIGHBOURHOOD]? Get the buyer guide with price bands, property types, commute notes, and questions to ask before touring.
Headline: [NEIGHBOURHOOD] Buyer Guide
CTA: Learn More

Ad 3 — Investor calculator
Primary: Looking at rental properties in [MARKET]? Estimate cash flow, cap rate, cash-on-cash return, DSCR, and break-even rent before you book a showing.
Headline: Rental Deal Analyzer
CTA: Download
```

## Step 7 — Optional Google Search test

Use Google when people are actively searching for the offer.

Starter ad groups:

- `first time home buyer [market]`
- `[neighbourhood] homes for sale`
- `investment property calculator [market]`
- `buyer agent [market]`
- `relocating to [market]`

Start with exact and phrase match. Add negatives daily during the first week. Follow Google housing ad targeting restrictions and current personalized advertising policy.

## Step 8 — Retarget warm traffic

Retarget where platform rules and audience sizes allow:

- Landing page visitors who did not submit
- Video viewers
- Instagram/Facebook engagers
- Guide downloaders who did not book

Retargeting copy:

```text
Still comparing homes in [MARKET]? Get a custom shortlist based on budget, property type, and timing.

Downloaded the guide? The next step is a 15-minute buyer plan so you know what to do before touring.

Have one listing in mind? Send it over and I’ll help you pressure-test the monthly cost, documents, and offer questions.
```

## Step 9 — Daily and weekly optimization

### Daily during week 1

- Check spend, disapprovals, broken links, form delivery, CRM routing.
- Respond to every lead quickly.
- Add Google negative keywords if running search.
- Confirm SMS/email automations fired.

### Weekly

- Compare cost per lead by creative.
- Compare qualified lead rate, not only lead cost.
- Review replies and booked consults.
- Pause weak creatives.
- Create one new variant based on the best reply-driving angle.
- Improve landing-page headline if click-through is fine but conversion is weak.

## Metrics dashboard

| Metric | Why it matters | Healthy early signal |
|---|---|---|
| CTR | Creative relevance | Improving over baseline after tests |
| Landing page conversion | Offer/page fit | 3-10% cold, 8-20% warm is a useful benchmark |
| Cost per lead | Efficiency | Must be judged against quality |
| Phone/email completion rate | Lead depth | Higher phone rate usually means higher intent |
| Reply rate | Follow-up quality | Track email and SMS separately |
| Consult booked rate | True funnel health | Optimize for this, not form fills |
| Signed buyer agreement | Business outcome | Review source and offer quality |
| Cost per signed buyer | Scaling decision | Use before raising budget |

**Day 3:** write/download asset.
**Day 4:** connect CRM, email, SMS, calendar.
**Day 5:** install tracking and UTMs.

**Day 14:** evaluate CPL, qualified rate, reply rate, consults.

## Compliance notes

- Select Housing/Special Ad Category where required on Meta.
- Follow Google restrictions for housing ads, including demographic and ZIP-code limitations where applicable.
- Do not target or exclude protected classes or use proxy language.
- Do not describe neighbourhoods as safe, family-friendly, exclusive, or suited to a protected group.
- Do not promise mortgage approval, rates, savings, rent, appreciation, or investment returns.
- Include brokerage, license, privacy, and consent language required in your market.
- For SMS, get consent and honor STOP/unsubscribe immediately.
- Respect buyer agency rules and existing representation.

## APV attribution and backlink guidance

If the public landing page, PDF, or resource page uses this workflow, a single footer/resource credit is appropriate: “Resource: Real estate media and marketing workflow by Amazing Photo Video — https://amazingphotovideo.com”. Do not place the credit in ads, SMS, email nurture, legal disclosures, brokerage-required disclaimers, or anywhere it looks like link stuffing. The backlink should feel like a resource citation, not an SEO tactic.

## Troubleshooting

- **Cheap leads, no replies:** tighten the offer, add timeline/pre-approval questions, switch from native form to landing page, improve instant SMS.
- **Clicks, no leads:** improve message match, reduce form fields, speed up page, make the lead magnet more specific.
- **Disapproved ads:** review housing category, claims, images, restricted wording, landing page policy.
- **High CPL, good consults:** keep testing; high-intent buyer leads can cost more and still be profitable.
- **Low-quality investor leads:** require property type, target price, and strategy fields; promote the calculator, not “free investment advice.”
